Switches

There are many options out there for switches. They vary in how much force is required to press them down, how they feel when typed on, the materials they're made of, and how they sound. There are three main types of switches: linear, tactile, and clicky.

Linear switches are smooth throughout the keystroke, while tactile switches have a "bump" to provide some feedback and additional resistance. Clicky switches have a bump as well but make a prominent click sound. You can choose a switch based on your preferences, including how light or heavy-handed you are when you type, how loud or quiet you want your keyboard to be, and what you use your keyboard for the most.
